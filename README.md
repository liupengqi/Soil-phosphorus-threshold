## Overview

This repository supports a study that developed a physics-informed neural network framework (**PINNs-P**) to quantify crop-specific soil phosphorus thresholds across global croplands. The model integrates agronomic knowledge with machine learning to predict crop yield and phosphorus loss responses to soil available phosphorus (Olsen-P), and to derive:

- **ASPT**: agronomic soil phosphorus threshold  
- **ESPT**: environmental soil phosphorus threshold  

Using these thresholds, the study further developed a **threshold-driven optimal phosphorus management (TDOP)** approach to evaluate global phosphorus fertilizer demand and reduction potential under current and future soil phosphorus conditions. The manuscript reports large spatial variation in ASPT and ESPT, global maps at 5 arcmin resolution, and scenario-based estimates of fertilizer reduction potential. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

### 1. Code

The code in this repository is used for:

- compiling and preprocessing the field dataset
- training and evaluating the **PINNs-P** models
- benchmarking against alternative models (e.g. RF, DNN, LMM)
- simulating crop yield and phosphorus loss responses to Olsen-P
- estimating global ASPT and ESPT maps

The repository includes processed datasets and/or links to external sources used in this study. These data support:

- model training and testing
- global gridded simulations of soil phosphorus thresholds
- validation analyses
- phosphorus input optimization under current and future scenarios

- field experimental data used for model training and evaluation, including crop yield and phosphorus loss observations under different soil, climate, and management conditions
- soil Olsen-P datasets
- gridded global maps of agronomic and environmental soil phosphorus thresholds for different crops generated by the model

## Main methodological components

### PINNs-P model
Five PINNs-P models were developed to simulate crop yield and phosphorus loss responses to gradients of soil Olsen-P under varying soil, climate, and management conditions. The framework combines:

- a main neural network for prediction
- a parameter subnetwork for site-specific response parameters
- physics-informed constraints based on agronomic response functions
- automatic differentiation during model training

### Threshold estimation
The trained models were used to simulate yield and phosphorus loss responses along an Olsen-P gradient, from which:

- the **ASPT** was identified as the point where yield response begins to level off
- the **ESPT** was identified as the point where phosphorus loss increases rapidly

### TDOP framework
The **threshold-driven optimal phosphorus management (TDOP)** approach was then used to estimate grid-level phosphorus fertilizer requirements under:

- current soil phosphorus conditions
- future optimal soil phosphorus conditions

These simulations were used to assess the global potential to reduce phosphorus fertilizer use while maintaining agronomic and environmental targets. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}
